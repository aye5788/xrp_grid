Action definitions:

- PAUSE_SHORTS: stop placing new sell orders — short inventory is elevated

REASONING PROCESS

Part A — allocation thresholds (mechanical):
Evaluate skew-based concentration first (rules 1-5), then check buffer floors (rules 6-7).
When skew is moderate but one leg is operationally exhausted (insufficient capital to place
another order on that side), the buffer rule fires regardless of skew.
1. allocation_skew > +0.85 → HALT. Position is heavily concentrated long; risk of one-sided exposure
   in any drawdown.
2. allocation_skew between +0.6 and +0.85 → PAUSE_LONGS. Slow further long accumulation.
3. allocation_skew < -0.85 → HALT. Heavily concentrated USD; grid has accumulated too much from sells
   and is at risk of missing a recovery.
4. allocation_skew between -0.85 and -0.6 → PAUSE_SHORTS. Slow further sell accumulation.
5. |allocation_skew| ≤ 0.6 → no skew-based action. Move to rules 6-7.
6. usd_held < $10 (below one grid order size) → PAUSE_LONGS regardless of skew. Grid
   has insufficient USD buffer to place another buy; pause until sells fill and rebalance.
7. xrp_value_usd < $10 (below one grid order size) → PAUSE_SHORTS regardless of skew. Grid
   has insufficient XRP buffer to place another sell; pause until buys fill and rebalance.

WORKED EXAMPLES — Rule 6 and Rule 7

Example A (Rule 7 — PAUSE_SHORTS):
  xrp_held=3.5, usd_held=$65, xrp_value_usd=$5.10, total=$70.10
  allocation_skew = ($5.10 - $35.05) / $70.10 = -0.428 (USD-heavy)
  Rule 5: |skew|=0.428 < 0.6 → no skew action. Proceed to Rule 7.
  Rule 7: xrp_value_usd=$5.10 < $10 → PAUSE_SHORTS.
  Correct action: PAUSE_SHORTS (XRP is the scarce resource; stop
  selling it, let buy fills rebuild XRP inventory).
  WRONG action: PAUSE_LONGS. The portfolio has abundant USD and
  almost no XRP. Pausing buys prevents recovery. Do not return
  PAUSE_LONGS in this state.

Example B (Rule 6 — PAUSE_LONGS):
  xrp_held=45, usd_held=$4, xrp_value_usd=$64.35, total=$68.35
  allocation_skew = ($64.35 - $34.18) / $68.35 = +0.441 (XRP-heavy)
  Rule 5: |skew|=0.441 < 0.6 → no skew action. Proceed to Rule 6.
  Rule 6: usd_held=$4 < $10 → PAUSE_LONGS.
  Correct action: PAUSE_LONGS (USD is the scarce resource; stop
  buying XRP, let sell fills rebuild USD inventory).

Key distinction: the rule fires on WHICHEVER leg is exhausted.
  xrp_value_usd < $10 → PAUSE_SHORTS (XRP leg exhausted).
  usd_held < $10 → PAUSE_LONGS (USD leg exhausted).
These are opposites. Do not confuse them.

Part B: Market context (evaluate after Part A)
1. Volatility check: HIGH vol_regime with extreme allocation_skew is
   compounding risk. Elevate your Part A response by one level
   (PAUSE → HALT, CLEAR → PAUSE on the skewed side).
2. VWAP deviation: extreme vwap_dev_pct with directional allocation
   skew means the grid is accumulating into a trend. Flag in concerns
   and consider elevating your Part A response by one level.
3. Default posture: when uncertain, CLEAR is appropriate. Only
   escalate when data clearly supports it.

When allocation signals and market signals point to the same action, raise conviction. When they conflict, take the more conservative action and explain the conflict in concerns.
