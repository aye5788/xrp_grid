## Pattern 1 — Directional Divergence and low atr_percentile are CONVICTION modifiers, not Step 1 vetoes
Observed 2026-05-17. Real insight retained from earlier reflection: when adx directional pressure (adx_pos vs adx_neg) contradicts the EMA stack direction (e.g. adx_pos > adx_neg in a bearish stack) AND atr_percentile is low (<15), a "trend" can be exhausted residual rather than active. That is captured cleanly by persona Step 1 condition 4 (MOMENTUM CONFIRMATION).

Decision rule (persona-aligned):
- Step 1 conditions 1-3 fire (deep EMA distance, aligned stack, price matches) AND condition 4 fires via ANY of {roc_6h matching trend sign at |0.3|, adx_pos/adx_neg matching, autocorr_1h > 0.15} -> TRENDING.
- Conditions 1-3 fire WITHOUT condition 4 -> STALE STRUCTURAL STATE -> fall through to Step 3 RANGING. This is the "exhausted drift" case.
- Directional Divergence and low atr_percentile reinforce the STALE diagnosis when condition 4 fails. They do NOT override condition 4 when it IS firing.

Common error to avoid (observed in prior cycles): voting RANGING when roc_6h has crossed -0.3 or +0.3 in the same direction as the EMA stack. That is active momentum confirmation — Step 1 fires and TRENDING is correct, even at low ADX and low atr_percentile.

## Pattern 2 — ROC Magnitude vs Grid Spacing
Observed 2026-05-17. When TRENDING is triggered via Step 1.4a (ROC confirmation) but the magnitude of the ROC (e.g., 1.0%) is significantly smaller than the grid's 'spacing_pct' (e.g., 2.5%), the 'trend' may not produce fills or pose immediate risk. In these cases, conviction should be capped at 0.7 to reflect that the directional move is not yet 'active' enough to dominate the grid's ranging mechanics.
